Ibadan, Nigeria

Nigeria, officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, is a West African country with a population of approximately 213 million. It is rich in cultural diversity, housing over 250 ethnic groups and major languages including Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo, Fulani, and Ibibio. The nation's culture is a blend of traditional and modern practices. Historically, Nigeria has a rich past marked by ancient civilizations such as the Nok culture, which dates back to around 1000 BC. The region saw the rise and fall of several powerful kingdoms and empires, including the Hausa city-states, the Yoruba kingdoms, and the Igbo societies. Nigeria was colonized by the British in the late 19th century and gained independence in 1960. Since then, it has developed into a significant economic and cultural hub in Africa.
